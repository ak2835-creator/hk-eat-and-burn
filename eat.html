Restaurants

Yat Lok (Central)

Yat Lok is one of Hong Kong's most respected roast goose restaurants, offering a focused menu and a distinctly local dining experience. The pace is brisk, the atmosphere is unpretentious, and the food is exactly what many visitors hope to find when they seek out a classic Hong Kong meal.

Details

Location
Central, Hong Kong
Price range
$$
Why it's special
Known for its roast goose, long-standing reputation, and its place in the city's Cantonese roast meats tradition.

Tim Ho Wan (Sham Shui Po)

Tim Ho Wan is a reliable dim sum stop for travelers who want something both famous and accessible. The setting is casual, the service is efficient, and the food reflects the kind of everyday Hong Kong eating that remains deeply satisfying.

Details

Location
Sham Shui Po, Kowloon
Price range
$
Why it's special
Widely recognized for offering well-made dim sum at approachable prices.

Kam's Roast Goose

Kam's Roast Goose is a polished but still firmly local Cantonese restaurant known for precise preparation and strong roast meat flavors. It is an excellent choice for travelers who want a meal that feels iconic without being overly formal.

Details

Location
Hong Kong Island
Price range
$$
Why it's special
Especially valued for its roast goose and strong reputation among Hong Kong roast meat destinations.

Lau Sum Kee

Lau Sum Kee offers a more traditional and understated experience, centered on handmade noodles and classic local comfort food. It suits visitors who want to eat well while staying close to the everyday rhythm of the city.

Details

Location
Sham Shui Po, Kowloon
Price range
$
Why it's special
Represents a more old-school side of Hong Kong dining, with handmade noodles as its main draw.
